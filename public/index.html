Why teams pick Tasker

Built for the gap between spreadsheets, cloud trackers, and enterprise workforce suites

Tasker is not a rostering platform and it is not a generic SaaS timer. It is a focused operational tool for understanding workload safely, quickly, and on your own infrastructure. Its design is aligned with peer-reviewed primary-care evidence on rising consultation burden and time pressure in general practice (Hobbs et al., Clinical workload in UK primary care: a retrospective analysis of 100 million consultations in England, 2007–14, The Lancet, 2016; Irving et al., International variations in primary care physician consultation time: a systematic review of 67 countries, BMJ Open, 2017).

Manual logs and spreadsheets

Low cost, but slow to maintain, easy to abandon, and weak for trend analysis or consistent categorisation.

Generic cloud time trackers

Polished reporting, but usually tied to email identities, vendor-hosted storage, and non-healthcare workflows.

Enterprise workforce suites

Strong for rostering and compliance, but too heavy when the immediate need is lightweight task-level workload evidence.

Tasker

Anonymous, mobile-first workload logging with analytics, exports, admin controls, and privacy documentation already built in.

What the app actually does

Task logging, interruption evidence, analytics, and rollout controls in one place

Log work live

Start a task when work begins, stop it when it ends, assign outcomes, and keep category choices fast with searchable comboboxes and quick picks.

Measure hidden workload

Capture interruptions and pending-task snapshots so teams can evidence hidden coordination work and work intensification, not only direct task minutes.

Review and export evidence

Use session and 30-day analytics, trendlines, and XLSX exports to support audits, service redesign, appraisals, and workforce planning.

Roll out safely

Admins can manage groups, dropdowns, notices, messages, backup/restore, registration rules, optional Turnstile CAPTCHA, and admin email 2FA.

Core operating principles

The product is designed around privacy, evidence, and low-friction adoption

Privacy first

  • Generated usernames instead of named user profiles
  • No patient data or real-name workflow required
  • 30-day retention to keep data exposure small

Evidence over anecdote

  • Live timing with structured outcomes
  • Pending workload snapshots and interruption logging
  • Analytics and exportable reports for decision-making

Simple deployment

  • Installable standalone web-app for mobile-friendly access
  • Guide, help page, policy, and DPIA published alongside the app

Common questions

FAQ

Is Tasker a clinical record system?

No. It is a workload-logging tool for operational evidence. It should not be used to store patient records or identifiable clinical information.

Can standard users stay anonymous?

Yes. The app is built around generated usernames and avoids requiring real names or email addresses for ordinary user accounts.

Where is the data kept?

On the organisation’s own infrastructure, under local governance, rather than relying on a third-party hosted database service.

What helps rollout and governance?

Tasker ships with an installation guide, user help, public policy, DPIA, admin controls, backup/restore tooling, and configurable registration rules.
